## 🎯 Objective
To consolidate, clean, and analyze a fragmented raw dataset to provide a unified view of PepsiCo's digital marketing performance, enabling data-driven decisions for future campaign strategies.

## 🛠️ Tools Used
* **Microsoft Excel:** Used for end-to-end data processing, analysis, and insight generation.

<a href=*https://github.com/reachsandip1-web/PepsiCo.-Social-Media-Analytics-for-Strategic-Branding/blob/main/Social_Media_Analytics_Raw_Dataset.xlsx*>DataSet</a>
  
## ⚙️ Methodology & Tasks Performed
The analysis was broken down into six core phases to extract actionable insights from the raw dataset:

1. **Data Preprocessing and Cleaning:** * Handled missing values, removed duplicates, standardizing data formats across different social media sources to create a unified and reliable dataset for analysis.

2. **Engagement Analysis:** * Evaluated core engagement metrics (likes, comments, shares, views) to determine how consumers interact with PepsiCo's content across different stages of the marketing funnel.

3. **Social Media Platform Analysis:** * Conducted a comparative analysis of Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, and YouTube to identify which platforms yield the highest visibility and engagement rates.

4. **Hashtag & Content Strategy:** * Analyzed the performance of specific hashtags, media types (video vs. image), and content themes to identify the most effective drivers of organic reach.

5. **Campaign Effectiveness:** * Measured the Return on Investment (ROI) and cost-efficiency of digital advertising efforts to help the marketing team optimize future ad spend.

6. **Follower Retention & Audience Loyalty:** * Examined follower growth trends, repeat interactions, and audience behavior to gauge long-term brand loyalty rather than just short-term campaign spikes.

## 💡 Key Insights & Recommendations
* *Platform Dominance: Facebook emerged as the top-performing channel overall, capturing the highest aggregate engagement volume across all measured platforms.

Content Format Efficacy: Static image posts consistently outperformed other media types on both Instagram and Facebook, driving peak interaction rates (likes, comments, and shares).

Dynamic Media on Twitter: Video campaigns demonstrated significant traction on Twitter, accumulating substantial view counts and indicating a strong audience preference for video content on this specific platform.*
